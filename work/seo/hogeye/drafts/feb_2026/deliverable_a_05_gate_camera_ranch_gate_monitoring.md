# Trap Compatibility and Warranty: Choosing a Trap-Ready Camera System

Trap performance depends on more than camera specs. For real field outcomes, system compatibility and support reliability matter just as much as image quality.

## Why compatibility matters

A trap-ready system should work across common trap formats without custom reinvention for every deployment. Compatibility reduces setup friction and lowers trigger-risk during active windows.

## Why support and warranty matter

When a system fails during an active period, response speed matters. Warranty and support are not "nice to have" features; they are uptime controls that protect capture opportunities.

## Modified-camera risk in trap operations

Modified or repurposed security setups can create hidden operational risk:

- inconsistent trigger response
- field failures with no practical support path
- unclear responsibility when components fail

For trap workflows, that risk translates directly into missed sounders and wasted labor.

## Selection checklist for trap teams

- Does the system support remote trap monitoring and closure?
- Is trigger timing reliable in your field conditions?
- Can multiple users monitor and trigger when needed?
- Is there a real support and warranty path for active-season failures?
- Does setup map to your existing trap operation process?
